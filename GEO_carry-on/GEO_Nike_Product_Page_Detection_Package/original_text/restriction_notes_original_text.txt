Restriction and Filtering Notes

Purpose
This folder stores the original raw text captures for reproducibility. These files should be preserved exactly as the scraped or copied text appeared in the working example.

Important restriction
Do not run cosine similarity directly on the raw original_text files as the main detection result. Raw text may contain scraping noise, form artifacts, duplicated product paragraphs, UI labels, and non-comparable page fragments.

Recommended comparison input
Use the files in the filtered_text folder for cosine similarity and feature-level comparison.

Filtering rule
The filtered_text files keep only same-product, same-topic, product-specific content. They remove or de-emphasize:
- form artifacts such as "Top of Form" and "Bottom of Form"
- low-information UI labels such as "Add to Bag" and "Favorite"
- duplicate repeated product descriptions
- repeated navigation-like labels
- unrelated or potentially scraped non-product text
- boilerplate that does not describe the target product or purchase support

What should remain
The filtered text should preserve:
- product name
- product category
- price
- color
- style code
- available sizes
- size and fit guidance
- material and construction details
- cushioning and comfort details
- outsole, traction, durability details
- product details
- shipping, returns, pickup, and payment information
- review count, rating, review snippets, and review summaries when present

Interpretation restriction
A higher similarity between the current page and a GEO-reference rewrite should be interpreted only as suggestive evidence of movement toward GEO-consistent product-page structure. It is not evidence that Nike or any retailer intentionally adopted GEO.
