The comparative analysis uses the numerical priority score as the primary inclusion criterion. The original categorical priority label is not used for analysis. This avoids inconsistencies between manually assigned labels and the score rubric. The score rubric is interpreted as follows: 9–10 = must-read / central paper, 8–8.9 = highly relevant and should be included in the memo, 7–7.9 = useful supporting paper, 6–6.9 = background only, and below 6 = mention briefly or exclude.

Analytical framing. The 23 papers should not be compared by headline performance gains alone. They measure different units: webpages, retrieved documents, products, generated claims, citations, page blocks, user preferences, and live search-result sets. The analysis therefore uses benchmark type, metric family, evaluation unit, and limitation category to make comparisons meaningful.

3. Paper Taxonomy and Evidence Types

The papers fall into distinct evidence types. The key methodological point is that GEO optimization papers, live platform audits, RAG benchmarks, multimodal document benchmarks, and temporal robustness papers answer different empirical questions.

Optimization GEO and website adaptation Papers 1, 2, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, and 9 ask whether content, structure, or evidence ecosystems can be optimized for AI visibility.
Measurement Live AI search / platform audit Papers 10, 11, 12, 13, and 14 measure how deployed or user-facing AI search systems select, cite, and present sources.
Evaluation RAG and citation quality Papers 16, 17, 21, 22, and 23 evaluate retrieval quality, source support, noisy context, outdated information, and citation fidelity.
Finance Financial and multimodal documents Papers 18, 19, and 20 connect AI search evaluation to financial statements, reports, slide decks, tables, charts, and visual citations.
Temporal Freshness and outdated evidence Papers 21 and 22 isolate a major risk for finance/econ applications: AI systems may retrieve or rely on outdated facts.
Bridge Finance/econ opportunity The strongest project bridge is converting technical AI visibility metrics into firm, product, publisher, and platform outcomes.

Comparison rule. Headline gains should not be compared directly across benchmarks. A 35% visibility improvement, a product-rank gain, an AIO activation rate, a citation-recall score, and a retrieval Recall@10 score are measuring different levels of the AI-search process.

5. Metric Crosswalk for Finance/Econ Variables

The main value of the CS literature for a finance/econ project is the menu of measurable technical outcomes. These measures can be converted into dependent variables for firm-, product-, publisher-, or platform-level analysis.

Metric family Example metrics Representative papers Finance/econ interpretation
AI visibility / impression Visibility score, impression score, position-adjusted word count 1, 4, 5, 6, 8 How much exposure a source receives inside an AI-generated answer.
Stage-level search visibility Retrieval hit rate, reranking rank, generation citation rate 2 Whether a firm or source is lost at retrieval, reranking, or generation.
Product / recommendation ranking Rank change, target recommendation, recommendation probability 7, 9 How AI-mediated recommendation changes seller or product visibility.
Source selection / overlap Citation count, Jaccard similarity, rank-biased overlap 3, 10, 11, 13 How much AI search diverges from traditional search and concentrates attention.
Citation absorption / influence Absorption score, source influence, answer-source semantic influence 3 Whether a cited source actually shapes the final answer.
Citation fidelity Citation precision, citation recall, unsupported-claim rate, contradiction rate 11, 23 Whether AI-generated claims are supported by cited sources.
User preference Preference votes, Bradley-Terry scores, citation-related coefficients 12, 13 Whether source presentation affects perceived credibility and user choice.
Temporal freshness Outdated-information harm, date-specific accuracy, dynamic QA accuracy 21, 22 Whether AI systems use stale firm, product, regulatory, or market information.
Visual citation Page-level precision/recall, block-level precision/recall 19, 20 Whether AI systems can cite the correct financial report page, chart, table, or block.

7. Limitation Heatmap Summary

The limitation analysis converts paper-level open gaps into comparable categories. This highlights a central tradeoff: controlled GEO benchmarks have better experimental control but weaker external validity, while live platform audits have stronger real-world relevance but weaker causal identification.

Limitation category Most affected papers Why it matters for finance/econ work
No direct economic outcomes Most papers Clicks, sales, conversion, ad revenue, publisher traffic, and firm outcomes are rarely observed directly.
Simulated / benchmark setting 1, 2, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9 Optimization gains may not transfer cleanly to live ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, or Google AIO behavior.
Observational / non-causal audit 10, 11, 12, 13, 14 Platform audits show what happens, but usually cannot identify the causal effect of specific website changes.
LLM-as-judge dependence Several benchmark papers Evaluation may inherit model bias, prompt sensitivity, and reproducibility concerns.
No content-intervention test 10, 11, 12, 13, 14 Live audits rarely test whether a concrete page rewrite or schema change increases AI visibility.
Platform instability 10, 11, 14, 21, 22 AI search systems change quickly, so measured visibility may be time-sensitive and difficult to replicate.
Limited finance linkage Most non-finance papers Only a subset directly addresses financial statements, firm disclosures, investor documents, or market outcomes.

8. Finance/Econ Research Gaps

The strongest contribution for a finance/econ project is not to replicate existing GEO benchmarks, but to link AI visibility to economic incentives and outcomes. The table below translates technical gaps into empirical research opportunities.

Gap area What CS literature covers Missing econ outcome Possible empirical design
AI visibility → economic outcome Citation, visibility, rank, source overlap Traffic, conversion, sales, ad revenue Build a firm/product panel of AI visibility across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Google AIO, and Google Search.
Firm website adaptation Content rewriting, structure, schema-like signals, GEO strategies Real firm behavior after AI search adoption Track FAQ pages, schema markup, headings, evidence density, and investor-relations pages over time.
E-commerce competition AI-mediated product rank and recommendation visibility Seller sales, conversion, marketplace competition Compare AI product ranking with traditional marketplace rank and product attributes.
Publisher impact AIO source selection, citation concentration, claim fidelity Referral traffic, ad revenue, publisher bargaining Link AI citations and AIO presence to publisher traffic proxies and ad-tech exposure.
Financial disclosure visibility Financial-document RAG, visual citation, long-context evaluation Investor attention, disclosure strategy, firm-level information access Test whether AI systems retrieve and cite 10-Ks, annual reports, prospectuses, and investor slides correctly.
Temporal misinformation Outdated information benchmarks and daily-changing QA Stale company, market, regulatory, or macroeconomic information risk Monitor AI answers to time-sensitive finance prompts over repeated collection windows.

9. Recommended Empirical Designs

Design 1 AI visibility panel Query ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Google AI Overviews, and traditional Google Search using standardized firm, product, and financial-information prompts. Record citation, rank, answer inclusion, source overlap, and citation position.
Design 2 Website adaptation study Use archived webpages or repeated crawls to track changes in FAQ design, schema markup, headings, evidence density, product descriptions, and investor-relations formatting after AI search adoption.
Design 3 Financial disclosure retrievability Test whether AI systems correctly retrieve, cite, and summarize 10-Ks, annual reports, earnings slides, tables, charts, and prospectuses. Evaluate page-level and block-level citation accuracy.
Design 4 Publisher and platform impact Measure whether AI Overviews and AI-search citations displace traditional search exposure. Link source visibility to publisher category, credibility, ad exposure, and potential traffic proxies.
Bottom-line research opportunity. The CS literature has developed strong technical measures of AI visibility, retrieval, citation, and RAG quality. The finance/econ contribution is to connect those measures to economic behavior: firm adaptation, product competition, publisher incentives, consumer attention, platform power, and information disclosure.
