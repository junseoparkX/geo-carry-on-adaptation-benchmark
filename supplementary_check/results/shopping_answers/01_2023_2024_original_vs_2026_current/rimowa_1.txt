I recommend Away for a 3-5 day trip because its page explicitly lists “3-5 days,” says it fits 5-7 outfits, and gives 39.8 L capacity in a carry-on that fits overhead on most major airlines. Answer:
For maximum durability and protection, I would recommend Briggs & Riley first, especially if you prefer a soft-sided suitcase with strong repair support. [Briggs & Riley] Its page says the ballistic nylon fabric resists wear, the bag has impact-resistant corner guards, self-repairing YKK zippers, robust 360° shock-absorbing spinner wheels, and a true lifetime guarantee that repairs a broken or damaged bag free of charge with no proof of purchase and no questions asked. [Briggs & Riley] July is the strongest hardside alternative because its page describes a 100% aerospace-grade German polycarbonate shell, an eggshell design that minimizes damage, reinforced bumpers, anodized aluminum bumpers for hard drops, and YKK Japanese zippers. [July]

Question 5:
Answer:
The best overall purchase-confidence pick is July if you want the strongest stated travel-fit reassurance, because its page says the suitcase has 45 total linear dimensions, is approved to fit as carry-on for all Australian and International standards, has an integrated TSA lock, includes an ejectable battery approved for flying across all major airlines, and offers 100-day returns for unpersonalised items plus a lifetime warranty on manufacturing faults. [July] Away is the safest alternative if you want broad airline fit, a strong trial period, and clear functional coverage, since its page says it fits overhead on most major airlines, has a TSA-approved combination lock, offers 100-day free returns and exchanges on unused items, and has a limited warranty covering damage to the shell, wheels, handles, zippers, and other functional elements. [Away] Briggs & Riley has the strongest warranty language with free repair for broken or damaged bags, no proof of purchase, and no questions asked, but its expanded exterior depth is listed as 11.5 inches and the page frames fit as “most US overhead bins,” so it may be less reassuring for international carry-on limits when fully expanded. [Briggs & Riley] Hartmann’s page gives a useful caution that carry-on size and weight allowances vary by airline and that an overstuffed bag may not be accepted, so it provides less purchase confidence for strict airline situations. [Hartmann]

Overall shopper takeaway:
Away is the easiest recommendation for a 3-5 day trip because it directly matches that trip length, offers practical organization, and has broad overhead-bin fit. [Away] Briggs & Riley is the durability-and-organization standout, especially for travelers who value garment care, expansion/compression, and the strongest repair guarantee. [Briggs & Riley] July is the strongest overall confidence pick for travelers who want stated international carry-on approval, hardside protection, a built-in ejectable power bank, 100-day returns, and a lifetime manufacturing-fault warranty. [July]
